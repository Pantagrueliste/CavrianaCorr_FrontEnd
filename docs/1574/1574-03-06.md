| **Summary** | Cavriana reports on the discovery of the conspiracy of the Duke of Alençon and the King of Navarre against Charles IX at Saint-Germain-en-Laye, tracing its origins to the time of the Admiral (Coligny) and involving the malcontents Méru and others. He describes the Queen Mother's reaction, the court's movements, the King's repeated offers of pardon, the role of La Mole, the involvement of Guise, Nassau's military threat on the Luxembourg border, Ottoman pressure on Philip II, and the prospect of a second St Bartholomew's Day massacre. |
